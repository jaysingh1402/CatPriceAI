PROPERTY CAT XL UNDERWRITING GUIDELINES — GULF COAST WIND & FLOOD
Version 3.2  |  Effective January 2024

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 1: INTRODUCTION & SCOPE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

These guidelines govern the underwriting of Property Catastrophe
Excess of Loss (Cat XL) treaties for cedants with exposure in
Texas, Louisiana, Mississippi, Alabama, and Florida.

Cat XL reinsurance is the primary mechanism by which primary
insurers manage their accumulation risk from a single catastrophic
event. Atlas Re provides capacity in layers from $10M to $500M.

SECTION 2: CEDANT ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

2.1 MINIMUM REQUIREMENTS
  • Minimum $100M TIV in the affected territory
  • At least 3 years of loss run history
  • A-rated carrier or Lloyd's syndicate
  • Must submit COPE data (Construction, Occupancy, Protection, Exposure)
  • Must maintain a licensed admitted status in all covered states

2.2 PREFERRED CEDANT PROFILE
  • Mixed portfolio across multiple states (not single-state concentration)
  • Less than 40% of TIV in any single county
  • Average property age < 20 years
  • Less than 50% wood frame construction
  • Less than 25% of TIV within 10km of coastline
  • Active cat model vendor relationship (RMS, AIR, or KCC)

2.3 INELIGIBLE BUSINESS
  • Mobile/Manufactured home portfolios >20% of TIV
  • Surplus lines business without admitted alternative
  • Beach & Windstorm Association business (state plans)
  • Portfolios with >30% Commercial Properties lacking sprinklers

SECTION 3: ATTACHMENT POINT GUIDELINES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

3.1 MINIMUM ATTACHMENT POINT
  Attachment should be set such that the cedant retains losses
  from all but the most severe events. Rule of thumb:
    Attachment ≥ 1-in-10 year PML (Probable Maximum Loss)

3.2 LAYER SIZING
  Maximum limit per layer: USD 100,000,000
  Minimum layer height:    USD 10,000,000
  Recommended thickness:   At least equal to attachment

3.3 PENETRATION PROBABILITY
  • First layer (closest to ground): max 20% annual penetration
  • Upper layers: expect < 5% annual penetration probability

SECTION 4: PRICING METHODOLOGY & BENCHMARKS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

4.1 PRIMARY PRICING TOOLS
  Atlas Re uses three methods with the following standard weights:
    • Burning Cost (Experience Rating):  30% weight
    • Frequency-Severity (Analytical):   45% weight
    • Exposure Rating:                   25% weight

  These weights may be adjusted where data quality warrants.

4.2 MINIMUM ROL STANDARDS (Gulf Coast, 2024)
  • Hurricane Wind:      Minimum 15% ROL for first-layer Cat XL
  • Hurricane + Flood:   Minimum 20% ROL for all-perils cover
  • Flood Only:          Minimum 8% ROL

4.3 EXPERIENCE PERIOD
  Minimum 5 years; preferred 10+ years.
  Post-2005 experience should always be included (Katrina benchmark).
  Events prior to 2000 receive a 50% weight due to portfolio change.

4.4 TREND ADJUSTMENT
  Apply 3% annual trend to historical losses for Gulf Wind.
  Apply 4% annual trend for Flood losses (NFIP reform impact).

4.5 CREDIBILITY STANDARDS
  • <5 years experience:   Use 0% credibility (exposure rating only)
  • 5-15 years:            Partial credibility (Bühlmann formula)
  • 15+ years:             Up to 70% credibility on burning cost

SECTION 5: EXPOSURE RATING PARAMETERS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

5.1 HURRICANE MEAN DAMAGE RATIOS (MDR) — Gulf Coast

  Construction Type     Zone A     Zone B     Zone C
  ─────────────────     ──────     ──────     ──────
  Wood Frame            4.5%       2.8%       1.2%
  Masonry               2.8%       1.8%       0.8%
  Steel Frame           1.5%       1.0%       0.5%
  Manufactured          8.0%       5.5%       2.5%

  Zones: A = <10km coast, B = 10-50km, C = >50km

5.2 FLOOD MDR ADJUSTMENTS
  FEMA Zone A:   add 2.5% to above MDR
  FEMA Zone AE:  add 1.5% to above MDR
  FEMA Zone X:   no additional loading

5.3 AGE FACTORS
  Built post-2000:  MDR × 0.80  (modern code compliance)
  Built 1980-2000:  MDR × 1.00  (baseline)
  Built pre-1980:   MDR × 1.20  (pre-code construction)

SECTION 6: RISK FLAGS & UNDERWRITING ACTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

6.1 AUTOMATIC FLAGS (require senior underwriter review)
  • Any single state concentration > 50% TIV
  • Any single county concentration > 20% TIV
  • Wood frame > 70% of TIV
  • More than 30% of TIV in FEMA high-risk flood zones
  • Technical ROL < market benchmark by more than 10%
  • Cedant loss ratio > 80% in any of the prior 3 years

6.2 ACCEPTABLE RISK MITIGANTS
  • Strong cat model output showing low modeled loss
  • Recent portfolio underwriting action (e.g., coastal non-renewal)
  • Third-party loss control certification
  • Multi-year agreement with rate step-up provisions

6.3 DECLINATION CRITERIA
  • No COPE data available
  • Cedant in regulatory/financial distress
  • Pending material litigation affecting the portfolio
  • Technical ROL < minimum ROL floor (see Section 4.2)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 7: IBNR AND LOSS DEVELOPMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

7.1 Cat losses typically develop faster than casualty:
  • 12 months: expect 75-85% of ultimate reported
  • 24 months: expect 92-97% of ultimate reported
  • 36 months: expect 98-100% of ultimate reported

7.2 Demand surge: add 10-15% to initial loss estimates
    for events with industry loss > $10B

7.3 Development-to-Ultimate factor for pricing:
    Apply a 1.05 factor to burning cost for late development.

SECTION 8: MARKET CONTEXT (2024 RENEWAL)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Post-Ian (2022), the Gulf Coast cat market has:
• Hardened significantly — average 30-40% rate increase 2023
• Reduced capacity from several large reinsurers
• Tightened terms: fewer Named Storm aggregate covers
• Higher minimum premiums and reduced reinstatements

Expected 2024 market ROL range (Gulf Coast All-Perils Cat XL):
• First layer ($25–50M xs $25M):    25–35% ROL
• Mid layer ($50M xs $50M):         18–25% ROL
• Upper layer ($50M xs $100M):      10–15% ROL

Recommended strategy: Quote competitive but firm; no sub-floor pricing.
